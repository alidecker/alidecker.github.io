Biography

Hello! Welcome to my website, which I coded as part of a computer science course at Tufts University.
I grew up in a small-ish town, and went to a private elementary and middle school for the arts. I was especially into the visual arts: painting and drawing were my best, although I did enjoy massaging clay into cool shapes as well. Outside of school, I played the piano and took dance lessons, and then in middle school I was turned on to cross-country, basketball and lacrosse. I have always been a math person, and loved to read.
In high school, my focus changed to more social ways to experience art. I joined the choir and loved it, stuck with sports (this time only lacrosse and ultimate frisbee) and dance, and pretty much left visual art and piano by the way side. I missed them, but I was so busy with my new interests and friends that it was hard to make time to draw, paint, and play the piano, all solitary activities.
So, in college, I made it my goal to really make time for piano and visual art. So far, I would say I've succeeded. I found Human Factors (a.k.a. Engineering Psychology), which engages visual art through design while also involving empirical research (which involves reading and math!). So, I get to do much art, math, and reading in my studies. I still do choir and dance, too - just less seriously than I used to.
The future, you ask? Well, I am currently a Senior, so I feel like I'm constantly talking to people (including myself) about my plans for next year, as well as for the next five or even ten years. I plan to find a job in User Experience (UX) design or research and work there for a few years. Then, I plan to go for a Master's degree once I have a better sense of what I want my career path to look like, after which I'll go back to work. Beyond that, I don't know, but I'm sure something good and meaningful will unfold. I'm looking forward to finding out what I'll do.
